1.2. Single-sided FFT (or Positive FFT)¶
    

  • Only want the first half of the FFT, since the last is redundant (symmetric)
    • 

  • 2 $\times$ amplitude except the DC component
    • 














In [5]:



    


% single-sides fft

xnt = fft(xn)/N;
xntss = xnt(1:N/2+1);
xntss(2:end-1) = 2*xntss(2:end-1);

3.2. Modulation and Demodulation¶
Modulation is the process of varying one or more properties of a periodic waveform, called the carrier signal, with a modulating signal that typically contains information to be transmitted. Most radio systems in the 20th century used frequency modulation (FM) or amplitude modulation (AM) to make the carrier carry the radio broadcast.

\end{align*}$$

Hilbert Transform


    

  • Do not worry about the equation. It will give us the envelope of a signal
    • 


$$H(u(t)) = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{u(\tau)}{t-\tau} d\tau$$











In [14]:



    


%plot -s 560,500

% extract envelope using Hilbert transform

xe = abs(hilbert(x));

subplot(2,1,1), plot(t,xe), xlabel('t'), ylabel('x(t)'), title('Extracted envelope'), ylim([-4,4])
subplot(2,1,2), plot(t,x), hold on, plot(t,[-1;1]*xe,'r','LineWidth',1), hold off, , xlabel('t'), ylabel('x(t)'), ylim([-4,4])

  • The spectral content of speech changes over time (non-stationary)
      

    • As an example, formants change as a function of the spoken phonemes
      • 

    • Applying the DFT over a long window does not reveal transitions in spectral content
      • 

    

    • 



    

  • To avoid this issue, we apply the DFT over short periods of time
      

    • For short enough windows, speech can be considered to be stationary
      • 

    • Remember, though, that there is a time-frequency tradeoff here!
      • 

    

    • 





    

  • Matlab function spectrogram is useful for easily computing STFTs
    • 














In [20]:



    


windowsize = 2^7;
window = hanning(windowsize);
nfft = windowsize;
noverlap = windowsize/2;

[S,F,T] = spectrogram(x,window,noverlap,nfft,Fs);
