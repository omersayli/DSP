1. Discrete Time Signals¶

A signal $x[n]$ is a function that maps an independent variable to a dependent variable.

In this course, we will focus on discrete-time signals $x[n]$:

  • Independent variable is an integer: $n \in \mathbb{Z}$
  • Dependent variable is a real or complex number: $x[n] \in \mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$

  • stem for discrete signals in MATLAB

2. Complex Signals¶
2.1. Complex Numbers¶





$$\text{Complex number = vector in 2D}$$

















2.1.1. Adding Complex Numbers¶


$$\begin{align*}
z_1 = a_1 + i b_1, \qquad  \vec{z}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}a_1 \\ b_1 \end{bmatrix}\\
z_2 = a_2 + i b_2, \qquad  \vec{z}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}a_2 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix}\\
\end{align*}$$


 
    

        
            
        
        
$$\begin{align*}
z = z_1 + z_2 &= (a_1 + a_2) + i (b_1 + b_2)\\ \\
\vec{z} = \vec{z}_1 + \vec{z}_2 &= \begin{bmatrix}a_1 \\ b_1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}a_2 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}a_1 + a_2 \\ b_1 + b_2 \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}$$
        
    




















2.1.2 Complex Exponential Function (Euler's Formular)¶


$$
e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta $$



 
    

        
            
        
        

$$\begin{align*}
\vec{z} &= r\cos\theta + i\,r\sin\theta \\
\vec{z} &= re^{i\theta} = r\,(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)\\ \\
r &\text{ : magnitude (length)}\\
\theta &\text{ : phase (angle)}
\end{align*}$$
        
    




















2.1.3. Multiplying/Dividing Complex Numbers¶
    

  • Just like an exponential function
    • 



 
    

        
$$
\begin{align*}
z_1 = r_1e^{i\theta_1} \\
z_2 = r_2e^{i\theta_2}
\end{align*}$$
        
        
$$
\implies$$
        
        
$$\begin{align*} z_1 \cdot z_2 & = r_1r_2 e^{i(\theta_1 + \theta_2)}\\
\frac{z_1}{z_2} & = \frac{r_1}{r_2} e^{i(\theta_1 - \theta_2)}
\end{align*}$$
        
    




















2.2. Circular Motion Represented by Complex Number¶
2.2.1. Geometrical Meaning of $e^{i\theta}$¶
$$
e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$$



 
    

        
            
        
        
$$\begin{align*}
e^{i\theta} & \text{: point on the unit circle with angle of } \theta \\
e^{i\theta} &=  e^{i(\theta + 2\pi)} = \cdots = e^{i(\theta + 2\pi k)} = \cdots\\ \\
&\text{static}
\end{align*}$$
        
    




















2.2.2. Geometrical Meaning of $e^{i\omega t}$¶
 
    

        
            
        
        
$$\begin{align*}
\theta &= \omega t = 2 \pi f t\\
e^{i\omega t} &: \text{ rotating on an unit circle with angular velocity of }\omega \\ \\
&\text{dynamic}
\end{align*}$$
        
    


Question: what is the physical meaning of $e^{-i\omega t}$ ?



















2.3. Sinusoidal Functions from Circular Motions (sin & cos)¶
    

  • Real part ($\cos$ term) is the projection onto the $Re\{\}$ axis
    

    • 

  • Imaginary part ($\sin$ term) is the projection onto the $Im\{\}$ axis
    

    • 






 
    

        
            $$\text{ projection of } e^{j \omega t} \text{ onto Re-axis}$$            
        
        
            $$\text{ projection of } e^{j \omega t} \text{ onto Im-axis}$$           
        
    

    

                    
                        
        
                    
                       
        
    

    

        
            $$\text{Re}\left(e^{i\omega t}\right) = \cos\omega t$$
        
        
            $$\text{Im}\left(e^{i\omega t}\right) = \sin\omega t$$

    •

3. Discrete Sinusoids¶


$$e ^ {j(\omega n+\phi)} = \cos (\omega n + \phi) + j \sin (\omega n + \phi)$$


Discrete Sinusoids

$$
\begin{align*}x[n] &= A\cos(\omega_0 n + \phi)\quad \text{or}\\ \\
x[n] &= Ae^{j\omega_0 n + j \phi}\end{align*}$$

















Key question: which one is a higher frequency?

$$\omega_0 = \pi ~~~ \text{or} ~~~ \omega_0 = \frac{3\pi}{2}$$
